MODULE 5 - LANs & WANs

Terms Definition
Attachment Unit Interface (AUI) Is a physical and logical interface defined in the original IEEE 802.3 standard for 10BASE5 Ethernet.
Jack The female component in a network device, wall outlet, or patch panel
Repeater Receives a signal. It can retime network signals at the bit level to allow them to travel a longer distance on the media
Passive A passive hub serves as a physical connection point only. It does not manipulate or view the traffic that crosses it.
Active It needs power to amplify the incoming signal before passing it out to the other ports.
Intelligent These devices basically function as active hubs. It also includes a microprocessor chip and diagnostic capabilities.
Wireless signals Are electromagnetic waves that travel through the air
Wireless networks use radio frequency (RF), laser, infrared (IR), satellite, or microwaves To carry signals between computers without a permanent cable connection.
Mobile Use Common application of wireless data communication
Transmitter Converts source data to electromagnetic waves that are sent to the receiver.
Receiver Converts these electromagnetic waves back into data for the destination.
Transceiver or wireless network card Many networking device manufacturers build the transmitter and receiver into a single unit.
RF technology Allows devices to be in different rooms or buildings
Single Radio Frequency Subject to outside interference and geographic obstructions.
Spread Spectrum Uses multiple frequencies to increase the immunity to noise and to make it difficult for outsiders to intercept data transmissions
NIC A printed circuit board that fits into the expansion slot on the motherboard or peripheral device of a computer
Peer-To-Peer Network Networked computers act as equal partners, or peers
Cisco 827 ADSL router Has one asymmetric digital subscriber line (ADSL) interface.
